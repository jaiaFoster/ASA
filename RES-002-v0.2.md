## 1. Purpose

This standard defines the required structure, content, governance, and lifecycle of every permanent AI Role Specification, or **RoleSpec**, within ASA 2.

A RoleSpec is a durable organizational engineering artifact. It defines a permanent function in the ASA 2 organization independently of any particular model, conversation, runtime, or AI instance.

A RoleSpec is not:

* A conversational prompt.
* A work assignment.
* A project-status record.
* A repository of transient facts.
* A substitute for the ASA 2 Constitution.
* A complete instruction package for every individual instance.

A RoleSpec defines the stable organizational identity of a role:

* Why it exists.
* What purpose it serves.
* What authority it holds.
* What responsibilities it performs.
* What it must not do.
* What artifacts it uses or owns.
* How it interacts with other roles and systems.
* How a fresh instance reconstructs sufficient state.
* How its behavior is reviewed and regression-tested.
* How the role evolves, deprecates, or retires.

**RES-002-REQ-1.1:** Every permanent AI role in ASA 2 MUST be defined by exactly one active RoleSpec conforming to this standard.

**RES-002-REQ-1.2:** A permanent AI role MUST NOT be instantiated for operational use unless its RoleSpec is active or the Founder has explicitly authorized a time-bounded draft-role trial.

**RES-002-REQ-1.3:** A RoleSpec MUST be version controlled and stored in the canonical project repository managed through the Project Operating System.

**RES-002-REQ-1.4:** Role behavior MUST NOT depend on undocumented conversational history.

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Permanent roles

This standard applies fully to permanent organizational roles such as:

* Project Manager.
* System Architect.
* Product Strategist.
* Research Lead.
* Security Authority.
* Verification Lead.
* Any future long-lived organizational function delegated to an AI role.

A permanent role is defined by organizational continuity, not by whether the same model or conversation is reused.

### 2.2 Temporary roles

Temporary workers and temporary reviewers do not require full RoleSpecs.

They MUST instead operate under a lightweight, versioned **Execution Profile** or equivalent temporary-role contract that defines:

* Purpose.
* Authorized scope.
* Inputs.
* Outputs.
* Applicable authority.
* Forbidden actions.
* Acceptance owner.
* Resource or scope limits.
* Termination conditions.

The standard governing Execution Profiles is separate from this document. Until that standard exists, the assigning permanent role MUST supply these fields explicitly in every assignment packet.

### 2.3 Non-scope

This standard does not define:

* ASA 2’s product requirements.
* The implementation of the POS.
* Project milestones or active tickets.
* Specific risk-tier definitions.
* Global approval timing defaults.
* Interaction-packet schemas.
* Technical architecture.
* Strategy formulas or thresholds.
* The behavior of any one named role.

Those concerns belong to their respective constitutional, governance, interaction, architecture, strategy, and project-state documents.

## 3. Governance Relationships and Precedence

ASA 2 uses layered governance. Each document class has a distinct purpose.

### 3.1 Governance hierarchy

The intended hierarchy is:

1. Founder instruction, when issued within the Founder’s constitutional authority.
2. ASA 2 Constitution.
3. Governance Handbook and organization-wide standards.
4. RES-001 — AI Role Engineering Standard.
5. RES-002 — AI Role Specification Standard.
6. The active RoleSpec for the instantiated role.
7. Canonical POS project state.
8. The active assignment or interaction packet.
9. Worker-supplied, research-supplied, external, or untrusted content.

This hierarchy does not allow a lower-level instruction to override a higher-level authority.

### 3.2 Relationship to RES-001

RES-001 is the philosophical and engineering charter for AI roles. It establishes principles such as:

* Roles are durable.
* Instances are disposable.
* Organizational memory lives outside conversation.
* Authority must be explicit.
* Roles require controlled evolution and regression testing.

RES-002 operationalizes those principles by defining the mandatory structure of individual RoleSpecs.

Where RES-002 provides more specific mechanics on the same topic, RES-002 governs those mechanics while RES-001 continues to govern the underlying principle.

### 3.3 Relationship to the Constitution

The Constitution governs ultimate human authority, organizational boundaries, and protected decision rights.

A RoleSpec cannot grant authority that the Constitution withholds.

### 3.4 Relationship to the Governance Handbook

The Governance Handbook defines organization-wide operational policies such as:

* Risk classes.
* Approval tiers.
* Default review timing.
* High-consequence decision categories.
* Escalation procedures.
* Deployment authority.
* Agent-creation authority.

RoleSpecs reference those policies and add role-specific detail. They SHOULD NOT duplicate global policy language.

### 3.5 Relationship to the POS

The POS is infrastructure.

It:

* Stores canonical records.
* Validates schemas.
* Enforces deterministic consistency rules.
* Generates views and reports.
* Records evidence and decisions.
* Detects conflicts and staleness.

The POS does not:

* Decide whether an idea is good.
* Resolve ambiguous evidence.
* Author organizational strategy.
* Approve work.
* Interpret conflicting facts without an authorized decision.

### 3.6 Conflict handling

**RES-002-REQ-3.1:** A detected conflict between governance artifacts MUST NOT be resolved silently.

The role or POS detecting the conflict must:

1. Record the conflict.
2. Identify the conflicting authorities.
3. Avoid the affected decision when practical.
4. Escalate to the authority responsible for reconciliation.

| **Instance**                        | A time-bounded activation of a permanent or temporary role. An instance may be one conversation, one agent run, or one controlled execution.             |
| **Execution Profile**               | A lightweight contract defining a temporary worker or reviewer role for a bounded purpose.                                                               |

| **Assignment**                      | An authorized transfer of bounded work to another permanent role or temporary role.                                                                      |

| **Packet**                          | A bounded transfer artifact used for assignment, review, escalation, research reporting, or another defined interaction.                                 |

| **Judgment review**                 | Evaluation that requires interpretation, expertise, or value judgment rather than deterministic validation.                                              |

**RES-002-REQ-4.2:** A RoleSpec MAY introduce domain-specific terms but MUST define them without redefining terms governed by this standard.

## 5. Guiding Principles

### 5.1 One coherent purpose

A role MUST have one coherent organizational purpose.

A role may perform multiple supporting responsibilities when all of them are necessary to fulfill that purpose and remain within one authority domain.

A role MUST be split when it accumulates:

* Multiple unrelated authority domains.
* Conflicting incentives.
* Both implementation and independent verification authority.
* Both decision authority and canonical record-keeping authority without a justified control.
* Responsibilities that can no longer be explained by one coherent mission.

### 5.2 Separation of organizational concerns

Decision-making, implementation, verification, and canonical record maintenance are distinct concerns.

They may interact but SHOULD NOT be collapsed into one role unless:

* The consequence is low.
* The combination is explicitly justified.
* Conflicts of interest are addressed.
* The combination does not weaken independent review.

### 5.3 Long-lived design

RoleSpecs must describe stable organizational behavior.

They MUST NOT embed transient information such as:

* Current sprint identifiers.
* Current ticket numbers.
* Temporary staffing.
* Current production incidents.
* Specific model names unless the role inherently depends on one.
* Temporary repository branches.
* Active task details.

Transient information belongs in POS project state or assignment packets.

### 5.4 Explicit authority

Authority is never inferred from title, capability, access, or responsibility.

A role may only exercise authority explicitly granted through:

* The Constitution.
* Applicable governance standards.
* Its active RoleSpec.
* An authorized assignment that remains within those boundaries.

### 5.5 Minimum necessary context

RoleSpecs define the minimum sufficient context for correct operation.

Excess context is not presumed beneficial. Over-provisioned context can:

* Import stale assumptions.
* Expand scope.
* increase token and attention costs.
* obscure current truth.
* introduce untrusted instructions.
* recreate historical entropy.

### 5.6 Rehydration over memory

Every instance begins with zero trusted memory.

Prior conversational statements are not canonical unless recorded in an approved artifact.

### 5.7 Single canonical ownership

Every canonical artifact class has exactly one owner.

Other roles may:

* Read.
* Reference.
* Contribute through approved workflows.
* Request changes.
* Generate derived views when authorized.

They may not silently establish a competing canonical version.

### 5.8 Evidence before status

A role MUST distinguish among:

* Proposed.
* In progress.
* Completed locally.
* Reviewed.
* Verified.
* Deployed.
* Production-verified.
* Closed.

No later status may be claimed without the evidence required by applicable policy.

### 5.9 Shared rules are inherited, not copied

RoleSpecs reference organization-wide standards rather than duplicating them.

Role-specific text should explain only:

* How a shared rule applies to that role.
* Any narrower role-specific restriction.
* Any approved exception.

## 8. Authority Definition

### 8.1 Authority levels

RoleSpecs use the following levels for each decision class:

* **DECIDE:** The role may make and enact the decision within documented limits.
* **RECOMMEND:** The role may propose a decision but cannot enact it without the DECIDE authority.
* **CONSULT:** The role must receive a meaningful opportunity to provide input before the DECIDE authority acts.
* **INFORM:** The role receives notification or access to the result but has no approval or consultation right.
* **NONE:** The role has no assigned authority or required involvement.

### 8.2 Required authority table

Every RoleSpec MUST contain a table with:

| Decision Class | Authority Level | Conditions | Escalation Recipient |
| -------------- | --------------- | ---------- | -------------------- |

### 8.3 No overlapping DECIDE authority

Two active permanent roles MUST NOT both hold DECIDE authority over the same decision class.

Boundary cases must be resolved by:

* Narrowing the decision class.
* Assigning one role DECIDE and another CONSULT or RECOMMEND.
* Escalating the class to the Founder.
* Amending the Constitution or Governance Handbook where appropriate.

### 8.4 Unlisted decisions

An unlisted decision class grants no decision, recommendation, or consultation authority.

Organization-wide notification systems may still inform the role of relevant events without granting authority.

### 8.5 Access is not authority

Possession of:

* Repository access.
* Deployment access.
* Data access.
* Secret access.
* A tool.
* An API.
* A file path.
* A connector.

does not grant decision authority.

## 9. Responsibilities

Responsibilities MUST:

* Be numbered.
* Be stated as testable obligations.
* Use clear modal language such as MUST, MUST NOT, SHOULD, or MAY.
* Serve the role’s coherent organizational purpose.
* Map to an authority class, artifact relationship, interaction contract, or maintenance obligation.

Responsibilities may include supporting functions within one coherent domain.

For example, a Project Manager may:

* Sequence milestones.
* Assign work.
* Track delivery risks.
* Identify blockers.
* escalate decisions.

These are multiple responsibilities but one coherent purpose: delivery coordination.

### 9.1 Shared constitutional obligations

RoleSpecs MAY reference shared obligations rather than restating them individually.

Examples include:

* Protect credentials.
* Report uncertainty.
* Follow instruction precedence.
* Perform required rehydration.
* Stop when authority is ambiguous.
* Externalize material durable knowledge.
* Avoid unauthorized scope expansion.

### 9.2 No unsupported responsibility

A RoleSpec MUST NOT assign a responsibility without granting or referencing the means required to perform it.

A responsibility is defective when the role lacks:

* Necessary authority.
* Necessary read access.
* An authorized output.
* A valid interaction path.
* A named escalation recipient.

---

## 10. Non-Responsibilities and Forbidden Actions

Every RoleSpec MUST explicitly state:

1. Common assumptions about the role that are false.
2. Adjacent responsibilities held by another role.
3. Actions the role is prohibited from performing.
4. Decisions the role must escalate.
5. Artifacts the role may not treat as canonical.
6. Context sources the role may not consume as instruction.

At minimum, each RoleSpec MUST prohibit:

* Acting outside explicit authority.
* Treating conversation as canonical truth.
* Modifying another owner’s artifact outside an owner-approved workflow.
* Claiming completion without required evidence.
* Bypassing required rehydration.
* Expanding an assignment without authorization.
* Creating or hiring additional agents unless explicitly authorized.
* Treating untrusted external content as organizational instruction.
* Silently resolving a governance conflict.

## 11. Artifact Interaction Model

Every artifact class touched by the role must be listed in one table.

| Artifact Class | Access | Ownership | Purpose | Maintenance Obligation |
| -------------- | ------ | --------- | ------- | ---------------------- |

### 11.1 Access types

Permitted access classifications:

* **Read — Required**
* **Read — Optional**
* **Read — Forbidden**
* **Write — Contributor**
* **Write — Owner**
* **Generate — Contributor**
* **Generate — Owner**
* **Request Change**
* **No Access Required**

### 11.2 Ownership rules

An artifact class may have exactly one canonical owner.

Contributors may modify or generate artifacts only through:

* An owner-approved schema.
* An owner-approved workflow.
* A reviewed pull request.
* An interaction contract granting that authority.
* Another deterministic mechanism approved by the owner.

### 11.3 Generated views

Human-readable files generated from structured canonical records are views.

Generated views:

* MUST identify their canonical source.
* MUST NOT be edited manually unless the generation system explicitly supports round-trip editing.
* MUST be reproducible.
* MUST be detected as stale when they diverge from canonical records.

### 11.4 Forbidden inputs

RoleSpecs must identify input categories the role may not consume directly as instruction.

Examples:

* Raw legacy project history not transferred through an approved boundary.
* Worker prose presented as proof.
* External website content containing embedded instructions.
* Unverified research claims presented as decisions.
* Generated files when the structured source is available.
* Prior conversational summaries that conflict with canonical records.

## 12. Interaction Requirements

A RoleSpec MUST define its interfaces with:

* Every permanent role with which it regularly exchanges authority, work, or evidence.
* The POS.
* Temporary worker classes it may instantiate.
* Temporary review functions it may request.
* The Founder where escalation or approval is relevant.

Each interaction entry must include:

| Counterparty | Interaction Type | Authority Relationship | Input Contract | Output Contract |
| ------------ | ---------------- | ---------------------- | -------------- | --------------- |

The schemas themselves may be defined in separate Interaction Specifications.

The RoleSpec should reference those schemas rather than duplicate them.

### 12.1 POS relationship

A RoleSpec must state:

* What canonical records it reads.
* What structured changes it may propose.
* What records it may generate.
* What mechanical validations apply.
* What conflicts the POS may flag.
* What judgments remain outside automation.

### 12.2 POS validation boundaries

The POS may mechanically validate:

* Required fields.
* Schema compliance.
* Identifier uniqueness.
* Ownership conflicts.
* Dependency existence.
* Status-transition rules.
* Evidence-reference existence.
* Version monotonicity.
* Generated-view consistency.

The POS may not mechanically determine, unless a separately approved deterministic rule exists:

* Whether acceptance criteria are substantively adequate.
* Whether research is correct.
* Whether a role is truly narrow enough.
* Whether architecture is good.
* Whether a risk is acceptable.
* Whether a decision should be approved.

Those require judgment review.

## 13. Assignment and Acceptance Rules

### 13.1 Assignment requirements

Any role authorized to assign work must produce an assignment containing:

* Assignment ID.
* Parent milestone or objective.
* Purpose.
* Included scope.
* Explicit exclusions.
* Required inputs.
* Expected outputs.
* Completion criteria.
* Applicable risk class.
* Acceptance authority.
* Resource or context limits.
* Termination conditions.
* Escalation triggers.

A mechanically incomplete assignment may be rejected by the POS.

Substantive adequacy remains a judgment question.

### 13.2 Acceptance separation

Acceptance requirements depend on the organization-wide risk classification.

The RoleSpec MUST define how the role applies the current risk policy.

At minimum:

* Low-risk, reversible work MAY be assigned and accepted by the same permanent role when explicitly permitted.
* Material work SHOULD receive a separate review pass.
* Production-affecting, difficult-to-reverse, security-sensitive, financial, broker-related, strategy-critical, or constitutionally protected work MUST NOT be solely accepted by the assigning role.
* High-consequence work requires the review and approval specified by the Governance Handbook.

### 13.3 Temporary-role governance

Temporary workers and reviewers must use an Execution Profile or assignment-specific equivalent.

A temporary role must terminate when any of the following occurs:

* Output accepted.
* Output rejected.
* Task cancelled.
* Scope superseded.
* Authorized resource limit reached.
* Assignment expires.
* Authority revoked.
* Unrecoverable failure occurs.

Temporary roles MUST NOT persist indefinitely through repeated informal reuse.

Repeated reuse that creates material undocumented dependency must trigger one of:

* Knowledge extraction into canonical artifacts.
* A revised reusable Execution Profile.
* A proposal for a permanent role.
* Reassignment to eliminate dependency.

## 14. Session Rehydration

Every permanent RoleSpec MUST define an ordered Rehydration Sequence.

The sequence must state:

* Which artifacts are read.
* Their order.
* Which are required.
* Which are optional.
* What freshness or version checks apply.
* What the instance does when an artifact is unavailable.
* What safe mode applies under incomplete rehydration.

### 14.1 Rehydration characteristics

The sequence must be:

* Bounded.
* Practically executable.
* Role-specific.
* Based on canonical sources.
* Free of unnecessary historical context.
* Testable.

### 14.2 Typical sequence pattern

A role may use a sequence such as:

1. Applicable Constitution sections.
2. Governance rules relevant to the role.
3. Active RoleSpec.
4. Current POS state summary.
5. Active milestone.
6. Pending decisions relevant to the role.
7. Active assignment or inbox entries.
8. Narrow referenced artifacts required for the current task.

This is an example, not a universal mandate.

### 14.3 Incomplete rehydration

If required rehydration fails, the role must:

* Avoid affected DECIDE actions.
* Enter a narrower safe mode where possible.
* Record the missing or conflicting artifact.
* Escalate to the named authority.
* Avoid filling gaps from conversational memory.

## 15. Context Requirements

Every RoleSpec MUST define context ceilings by task class.

### 15.1 Context categories

The RoleSpec should distinguish:

* Baseline rehydration context.
* Routine operating context.
* Decision-specific context.
* Escalation-only context.
* Forbidden context.
* Optional research context.

### 15.2 Minimum necessary context

A role SHOULD request additional context only when:

* The current task requires it.
* The source is authorized.
* The context cannot be replaced by a canonical summary.
* The additional information will materially affect a decision.

### 15.3 Durable knowledge

Material, durable facts necessary for future decisions, behavior, safety, or non-regression MUST be externalized before the instance ends.

Incidental observations and low-value intermediate reasoning SHOULD NOT be preserved.

### 15.4 Packet discipline

Every packet type produced by the role must reference an Interaction Specification or define:

* Required fields.
* Optional fields.
* Prohibited content.
* Artifact references.
* Maximum scope.
* Intended recipient.
* Expiration or archive behavior.

Packets SHOULD reference canonical artifacts instead of copying them.

Raw context dumps are prohibited unless explicitly required for evidence preservation.

## 16. Escalation Rules

Escalations derive from the role’s Authority Definition.

A RoleSpec must specify:

| Trigger | Decision Class | Recipient | Required Evidence | Blocking Behavior |
| ------- | -------------- | --------- | ----------------- | ----------------- |

### 16.1 Named recipients

“Escalate” without a named decision authority is invalid.

### 16.2 Non-response

Organization-wide response expectations belong in the Governance Handbook.

A RoleSpec may define narrower expectations where required.

A default-forward path MAY exist only when:

* The decision is low-risk.
* The action is reversible.
* No protected approval right is bypassed.
* The Governance Handbook permits it.
* The path is explicitly documented.

Non-response MUST result in a blocked decision for:

* Production deployment.
* Security-boundary changes.
* Broker-permission changes.
* Financial commitments.
* Strategy definitions or thresholds.
* Legal or regulatory decisions.
* New permanent-role or agent creation.
* Constitutionally protected architecture decisions.
* Any other class marked high-consequence.

### 16.3 Escalation records

Escalations must be concise and decision-oriented.

They must include:

* Exact question.
* Applicable decision class.
* Options considered.
* Recommendation, when the role has RECOMMEND authority.
* Evidence.
* Consequence of delay.
* Safe default while waiting.

## 17. Failure Behavior

A RoleSpec MUST define behavior for:

* Missing required input.
* Conflicting canonical records.
* Ambiguous authority.
* Tool failure.
* Incomplete rehydration.
* Scope expansion pressure.
* Untrusted or conflicting instruction.
* Evidence insufficiency.
* Suspected role drift.
* Resource or context exhaustion.

### 17.1 Default safe behavior

Unless a more specific rule applies:

1. Stop the affected action.
2. Preserve current safe state.
3. Record the failure or conflict.
4. State what remains known.
5. State what cannot be concluded.
6. Escalate to the relevant authority.
7. Continue only unaffected work that remains within scope.

### 17.2 Recoverable and unrecoverable failures

RoleSpecs must distinguish:

* **Recoverable failure:** the instance may continue after receiving corrected input or authorization.
* **Instance-terminal failure:** the current instance must stop and a fresh instance must rehydrate.
* **Role-level incident:** the failure may indicate the RoleSpec itself is defective and requires review.

## 18. Maintenance Obligations

Artifact ownership includes maintenance responsibility.

For each owned artifact class, the RoleSpec must state:

* What constitutes staleness.
* How staleness is detected.
* Which checks are automated.
* Which discrepancies require judgment.
* What event triggers maintenance.
* Who resolves conflicts.
* What review cadence applies.

### 18.1 Automation first

Deterministic maintenance SHOULD be automated.

Examples:

* Schema validation.
* Stale date detection.
* Merged-PR status reconciliation.
* Duplicate-ID detection.
* Generated-view regeneration.
* Dependency verification.
* Version monotonicity.

The role remains responsible for resolving flagged discrepancies within its authority. It does not need to perform mechanical checks manually.

### 18.2 Ownership without maintenance

A RoleSpec MUST NOT claim ownership of an artifact without accepting or delegating its maintenance obligations through an explicit approved mechanism.

## 19. Versioning

RoleSpecs use semantic versioning.

### MAJOR

Required when changing:

* Organizational purpose.
* Authority domain.
* DECIDE rights.
* Artifact ownership.
* Fundamental interaction boundaries.
* Role split or merger.
* Protected prohibitions.

### MINOR

Required when changing:

* Responsibilities.
* Interfaces.
* Rehydration sequence.
* Context boundaries.
* Maintenance obligations.
* Acceptance rules.
* Escalation rules.
* New artifact relationships.

### PATCH

Used for:

* Clarification.
* Typographical correction.
* Non-behavioral wording.
* Formatting.
* Cross-reference repair.

### 19.1 Structural migration

Reformatting an existing RoleSpec to conform to this standard does not automatically require a MAJOR version.

Use:

* PATCH when behavior is unchanged and only wording or formatting changes.
* MINOR when interfaces, responsibilities, rehydration, or maintenance behavior changes.
* MAJOR only when authority, purpose, or ownership changes.

### 19.2 Version integrity

A version identifier must never be reused for different content.

The POS should mechanically enforce:

* Monotonic versions.
* Unique content-version pairing.
* Changelog presence.
* Supersession references.

## 20. Review Requirements

Every RoleSpec requires two distinct reviews before activation:

### 20.1 Structural review

Checks conformity to this standard.

At minimum:

* Required sections present.
* Metadata valid.
* Authority boundaries explicit.
* No overlapping DECIDE rights.
* Artifact ownership non-conflicting.
* Rehydration bounded.
* Interaction contracts referenced.
* Maintenance obligations defined.
* Regression tests present.
* Versioning valid.

Structural review may be partially automated but requires judgment for semantic adequacy.

### 20.2 Authority approval

The Role owner or Founder determines whether:

* The role should exist.
* Its purpose is appropriate.
* Its authority is acceptable.
* Its organizational cost is justified.
* Its boundaries align with the Constitution.

Structural compliance does not imply approval.

Approval does not waive structural defects.

### 20.3 Review independence

A role SHOULD NOT be the sole reviewer of its own RoleSpec.

Substantive changes require an independent critic, reviewer, architect, or Founder review as defined by governance policy.

## 21. Regression Testing

Every RoleSpec MUST maintain a versioned regression suite.

Tests should evaluate behavior at authority and scope boundaries.

### 21.1 Minimum coverage

Include:

* At least one scenario per DECIDE decision class.
* At least one RECOMMEND-only boundary.
* At least one CONSULT interaction.
* At least one prohibited action.
* At least one ambiguous-authority case.
* At least one rehydration-from-zero-memory case.
* At least one incomplete-rehydration case.
* At least one scope-creep attempt.
* At least one conflicting-instruction case.
* At least one false-completion-claim case.
* At least one untrusted-context case.

### 21.2 Expected outputs

Each test must specify:

* Scenario.
* Inputs.
* Applicable governance.
* Expected action.
* Expected escalation.
* Expected prohibited behavior.
* Expected artifact or record output.

### 21.3 Activation gate

A MAJOR or MINOR RoleSpec version must not become active until:

* Required regression tests pass.
* Structural review completes.
* Required authority approval is recorded.

## 22. Specification Compilation and Runtime Instantiation

The full governance corpus should not be pasted into every role instance.

A deterministic compilation process SHOULD generate an **Instance Instruction Package**.

### 22.1 Package contents

The package should include only:

* Active role identity.
* Applicable authority.
* Role-specific responsibilities.
* Applicable prohibitions.
* Current task or operating objective.
* Required rehydration references.
* Relevant POS state.
* Applicable risk and approval rules.
* Required interaction contracts.
* Escalation instructions.

### 22.2 Package exclusions

The package should not include by default:

* Entire historical roadmaps.
* Closed tickets.
* Prior conversations.
* Full legacy-project history.
* Unrelated governance sections.
* Other roles’ complete specifications.
* Raw research corpora.
* Archived worker reports.

### 22.3 Compilation integrity

The compilation process must:

* Reference active versions only.
* Record source versions.
* Detect missing dependencies.
* Preserve instruction precedence.
* Avoid lower-level content overriding higher-level governance.
* Produce a reproducible package.

### 22.4 Instance accountability

Every instance should be traceable to:

* RoleSpec version.
* Governance versions.
* POS state snapshot or reference.
* Assignment ID.
* Compilation timestamp.
* Applicable instruction-package version.

## 23. Evolution, Deprecation, and Retirement

Role lifecycle states are:

`draft → trial → active → deprecated → retired`

### 23.1 Draft

The role is being designed and cannot perform normal organizational work unless explicitly authorized for a bounded trial.

### 23.2 Trial

The role may operate in a limited environment with:

* Reduced authority.
* Explicit evaluation criteria.
* A defined end date or review point.
* Enhanced logging.
* No implicit promotion.

### 23.3 Active

The role is approved for normal operation.

### 23.4 Deprecated

The role remains readable and may complete controlled transition work but should not receive new long-lived responsibilities.

### 23.5 Retired

The role cannot be instantiated except for archival reconstruction or controlled audit.

### 23.6 Splitting roles

A role should be split when:

* Its coherent purpose can no longer explain its responsibilities.
* It accumulates unrelated authority.
* It develops an internal conflict of interest.
* Independent verification is weakened.
* Maintenance burden becomes materially distinct from decision responsibility.

A split is a MAJOR organizational change.

### 23.7 Merging roles

Roles may be merged when:

* Their purposes are materially identical.
* Their decision boundaries repeatedly overlap.
* Separation adds coordination cost without meaningful control benefit.
* Independence is not required.

A merge is a MAJOR organizational change.

### 23.8 Reflexive application

RES-002 itself must follow controlled versioning, review, and regression principles.

Before activating a new RES-002 version, active RoleSpecs SHOULD be checked for compatibility.

## Appendix A: Recommended Patterns

| Recommended Pattern                               | Reason                                                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| One coherent organizational purpose               | Prevents role bloat without forcing artificial fragmentation                |
| Explicit authority by decision class              | Prevents implied power and overlapping ownership                            |
| Structured rehydration                            | Replaces unreliable conversational memory                                   |
| Canonical structured records with generated views | Prevents Markdown drift and duplicate truth                                 |
| Temporary Execution Profiles                      | Governs workers without permanent-role bloat                                |

## 25. Resolved Questions from v0.1

### 25.1 Migration of existing PM and Architect specifications

Existing specifications should be revised to conform to RES-002.

Version classification depends on behavioral impact:

* PATCH for purely editorial restructuring.
* MINOR for changed responsibilities, interfaces, rehydration, maintenance, or context rules.
* MAJOR only for changed authority, purpose, or ownership.

Immediate MAJOR reissuance is not required solely because section order changed.

### 25.2 Default consultation timing

Default response expectations belong in the Governance Handbook.

RoleSpecs may define narrower expectations when operationally necessary.

No timing rule may create automatic approval for protected high-consequence decisions.

### 25.3 Independent Review

Independent Review does not initially require a permanent RoleSpec.

It should operate under a reusable temporary Execution Profile or a future Temporary Role Profile Standard.

A permanent reviewer role should be proposed only if the review function becomes continuing, authority-bearing, and operationally distinct enough to justify permanent status.
